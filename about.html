My story, briefly

In 2004 I was bitten by a tick in the San Francisco Bay Area and told, flatly, that "Lyme doesn't exist in California." That single sentence cost me a decade. For ten years I went from specialist to specialist — neurologists, podiatrists, everything in between — while the disease progressed and my life shrank. I had to close a thriving business and leave my career in mental health just to care for myself.

In 2014, after moving to Colorado and finally finding the right physician and the right labs, I got my answer: CDC-positive Lyme. Then my daughter, my son, and my husband all tested positive too. Lyme, I learned the hard way, is a family illness.

In 2017, my husband James found whole-body hyperthermia, and our whole family traveled to Germany for treatment. To say it gave us our lives back would be an understatement. But I also learned that recovery isn't a straight line — clearing an infection isn't the same as being immune to it, and our family's journey has continued in ways that shape how I help people today. I don't speak about any of this from the sidelines. I'm still living it.

What I do — and how I'm paid

I guide patients and families through the overwhelming maze of chronic Lyme: understanding your options, making sense of treatments from whole-body hyperthermia to immunotherapy, and connecting you with trusted hospitals and clinicians around the world. I walk beside you from first contact through treatment and recovery.

For more on my background, credentials, and the guides I’ve written, see my author page.

My guidance is always free to you. The hospitals and programs I partner with compensate me only if you choose to move forward with treatment — so my only goal in our conversation is helping you find what's right for you, with zero pressure and nothing to sell.
